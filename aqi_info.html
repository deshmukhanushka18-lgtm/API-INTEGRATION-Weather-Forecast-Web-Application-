What is AQI?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical scale used to measure how polluted the air is and how it affects human health. A higher AQI value indicates poorer air quality and greater health risks.

AQI is calculated based on pollutants such as:

  • PM2.5 (Fine particulate matter)
  • PM10 (Coarse particulate matter)
